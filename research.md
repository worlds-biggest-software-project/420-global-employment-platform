# Global Employment Platform (Employer of Record)

**Date:** 2026-05-02
**Category:** HR Technology / Global Workforce Management

---

## Overview

A Global Employment Platform (GEP), also known as an Employer of Record (EOR) service, enables companies to hire and employ workers in countries where they have no legal entity—without needing to establish a local subsidiary. The EOR platform becomes the legal employer of record in each jurisdiction, handling employment contracts under local law, payroll processing in local currency, statutory benefit enrolment, tax withholding and remittance, and ongoing compliance with labour regulations. The client company retains day-to-day management of the worker's activities.

This model has become strategically important for two reasons: remote-first hiring has decoupled talent location from office location, and establishing a legal entity in a new country typically costs $20,000–$50,000 and takes six to eighteen months. EOR platforms compress that timeline to days and eliminate most of the capital and administrative overhead.

## Market Context

The global EOR market reached approximately $6.82 billion in 2025 and is growing at around 9% annually. The space is led by Deel (150+ countries, 35,000+ customers), Remote (90+ owned entities), Oyster HR (180+ countries), Globalization Partners (180+ countries), and Safeguard Global (187+ countries). Rippling entered the EOR market in 2023, leveraging its integrated HR/IT/finance platform to differentiate on workflow automation. Atlashxm and WorkMotion serve the mid-market. BambooHR added EOR as a module in 2024 for its existing customer base.

The dominant competitive dynamics in 2026 revolve around entity ownership (direct legal entities vs. partner networks, which affects compliance reliability and speed), pricing transparency, AI-assisted compliance monitoring, and the breadth of integrated services beyond core EOR (contractor management, equity administration, immigration support).

## Key Capabilities

### 1. Compliant Employment Contracts
Locally compliant employment agreements generated automatically based on jurisdiction, role, compensation structure, and applicable collective bargaining agreements. Contracts are e-signed and stored centrally, with version control tracking regulatory changes that require contract amendments.

### 2. Local Payroll Processing
Payroll calculated and disbursed in local currency with correct statutory deductions (income tax, social security, pension contributions, health insurance). Platforms such as Deel and Oyster support payroll in 140+ currencies. Automated tax filings and statutory reports are submitted to local authorities on behalf of the client.

### 3. Statutory and Supplemental Benefits Administration
Enrolment in mandatory benefits (national health insurance, pension schemes, mandatory leave entitlements) and optional supplemental benefits (private health insurance, life insurance, wellness allowances) configured per jurisdiction. EOR platforms maintain updated benefit catalogues as regulations change.

### 4. Compliance Monitoring and Alerts
Continuous tracking of labour law changes, tax rate updates, minimum wage adjustments, and new statutory requirements across all active jurisdictions. Automated alerts flag changes requiring contract amendments, payroll adjustments, or employee notifications before effective dates.

### 5. Onboarding and Offboarding Workflows
Digitised document collection (identity verification, right-to-work checks, tax forms), background check coordination, equipment provisioning integration, and compliant offboarding (notice period management, severance calculation, final pay processing) in each jurisdiction.

### 6. Contractor-to-Employee Conversion and Misclassification Risk
Tools to assess whether existing contractor relationships carry misclassification risk under local regulations, and structured pathways to convert contractors to employees through the EOR model. This is a high-value use case given aggressive misclassification enforcement in the EU, UK, and Brazil.

### 7. Equity and Expense Management
Integration with equity administration platforms (Carta, Shareworks) to handle international equity grants with correct tax treatment, and employee expense reimbursement workflows compliant with local rules on expense reporting and foreign exchange.

## Build Considerations

A new entrant in the EOR market faces exceptionally high structural barriers:

- **Legal entity network:** The core product is the network of local legal entities that underpin employment in each jurisdiction. Building this from scratch requires country-by-country legal setup, capitalization, local HR and payroll staff, and regulatory registration. Most new entrants start with a partner network and transition to owned entities over time—but partner-network models carry reliability and accountability risks.
- **Compliance infrastructure:** Labour laws change constantly across 180+ jurisdictions. Maintaining an accurate, up-to-date compliance engine requires dedicated legal and regulatory teams in each major market and systematic monitoring of legislative changes.
- **Payroll accuracy:** Payroll errors in international markets carry significant legal and reputational risk. The operational infrastructure for multi-currency, multi-jurisdiction payroll processing requires substantial engineering and human operations investment.
- **Differentiation:** The EOR market has commoditised at the product feature level. Differentiation in 2026 is primarily on: speed of onboarding (hours vs. days), the reliability and depth of owned entity coverage, AI-driven compliance monitoring, bundled services (immigration, equity, benefits), and integration with the client's existing HR stack.
- **Regulatory risk:** Several jurisdictions (Germany, France, Italy) impose restrictions on the use of EOR models and require careful structuring. An EOR platform must maintain country-specific legal guidance and flag jurisdictions where the model is restricted or prohibited.
